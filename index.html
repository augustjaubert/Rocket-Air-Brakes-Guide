What are air brakes?

Air brakes are a type of flight control surface mounted on a vehicle used to vary/increase its drag without significantly changing its lift, allowing for the vehicle to decelerate.

In many high power rocketry competitions, one of the grading criteria is based on achieving a predefined target altitude at apogee (highest altitude point in flight). The rocket flight can be separated into four stages: Launch and powered ascent, coasting period up to apogee, descent and landing. In the case of a model rocket braking system, only the launch and coasting stages are of interest.
